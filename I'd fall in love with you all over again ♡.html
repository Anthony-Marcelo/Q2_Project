Did you know that Kyoto used the capital of Japan?

In fact, it maintained that position for over 1000 years before Edo (which is now modern-day Tokyo), became the capital in 1868. There are also lots of temples and shrines in Kyoto that makes it attractive to those who wish to pay their respects to their gods or spirits. In Horimiya and in other Japanese media I've consumed (mainly anime and manga), whenever they head to Kyoto, the most common souvenir I've seen mentioned are sweets of some form. Kyoto seems to be renowed for preseving many of Japan's more traditional aspects, even translating to their sweets!

In terms of comparison, the anime definitely doesn't have the same amount of realism in its backgrounds compared to Dr. STONE but just like the other animes I mention, the potrayal of Kyoto fit really well in the anime while not clashing with the anime's aesthetic. It's very easy on the eyes while also being distinguishable as Kyoto.
