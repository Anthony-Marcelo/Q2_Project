Lastly, very popular anime currently airing, known for its insane animation, and has found its way in many's hearts and minds (including mine): JUJUTSU KAISÈN!

In particular, we're focusing on Season 2's 'Shibuya Incident' arc for our destination here. If you can guess by the name, this arc takes place in none other than the very popular Shibuya Crossing in Japan. JUJUTSU KAISÈN taking its unique twist on the modern human world with Jujutsu Sorcerers made to exorcise man-made curses. These curses, born out of negative emotions are how the show explains the countless unknown causes for deaths and disappearances in the show and goes through great lengths to represent realistic human emotions in all of its scenes (whether light-hearted or gut-wrenching).

For this arc in particular, on Halloween night in Shibuya, a weird barrier has trapped the humans who have come to Shibuya to celebrate Halloween. Our main characters go to Shibuya to investigate who had created the barriers only to be instantly faced with endless challenges and curses increasingly more powerful than the last.

JUJUTSU KAISÈN is so interesting because of how it potrays human responses to supernatural phenomena. The hopelessness, the fear, the fortitude, the despair, all of it is potrayed well enough to make you feel what the characters are feeling in that very moment. I also happen to have this anime as one of my favorites because I'm a big sucker for human psychology, especially the darker depths of the human mind.
