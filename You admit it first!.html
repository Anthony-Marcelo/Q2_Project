Before I ask, of course you know that Tokyo is Japan's capital.

So instead of doing the "Did you know" tatic to hook someone, let me just tell you about some interesting facts I've come to learn about Tokyo, それでは、始めましょう！

As you know from the previous photo album, Tokyo used to be called Edo which eventually transformed into "Toyo" during the Meiji Restoration. Even though its name translates to "Eastern Capital", no official laws have actually made Tokyo the capital of Japan and some Kyoto locals insist that Kyoto is still the rightful capital of Japan.

It's also home to the famous Shibuya Crossing, which I will expound unpon later in JUJUTSU KAISÈN.

Of course I can't forget the weirdness of the vending machines in Tokyo. There's even an Isekai anime (basically animes wherein the main character is reincarnated) where the main character turned into a vending machine! No longer is it the usual soda drink and quick snacks that take up the vending machine space. Want a hamburger? Here you go! It's raining? Umbrellas are just a vending machine away! Need condiments? Vending machine! It's actually impressive the variety of items you can get in these machines.

Now this might be a sudden mood shift but another thing I find interesting are the suicide lights installed in its Metro Stations. The color blue was proven to help bring people to a state of relaxtion quickly hence the suicide rate falling by as much as 84%. I wanted to point this out directly because suicide is personally a sensitive topic to me and it makes my heart sore that people out there are putting measures to prevent it.

Now, I find KAGUYA-SAMA: LOVE IS WAR interesting in how it potrays its backgrounds. Just like Horimiya, instead of using realism, it fits the surroundings into the anime's artstyle. One thing I'd say it's doing differently is that it's using it as a backdrop instead of having it be the main event. Personally, I like this little touch because it's like a nice little easter egg for the viewer to be able to recognize the real life references!
